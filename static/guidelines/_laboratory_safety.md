# Laboratory Safety Guidelines

> These guidelines integrate the lab's internal operating rules with safety requirements from institutional training courses and Italian legislation (D.Lgs. 81/2008, CLP Regulation 1272/2008). All personnel must read and follow these rules before working in the laboratory.

---

## 1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

### Always Required

- **Lab coat**: must be worn closed, with sleeves not rolled up and elasticised at the wrists.
- **Safety glasses** with side shields: mandatory for all laboratory operations. They protect against solid projections and small splashes.
- **Safety goggles** (mask type): required when there is a risk of large splashes, when handling corrosive materials, or if wearing corrective lenses.
- **Gloves**: must be selected based on chemical compatibility (see table below) and inspected for damage before every use. Single-use gloves must never be reused.

### Glove Selection by Chemical Type

| Glove Material | Suitable For | Not Suitable For |
|---|---|---|
| Nitrile | Alcohols, aromatic and chlorinated solvents | Concentrated oxidising acids |
| PVC | Acids, bases, aliphatic hydrocarbons | Ketones, most organic solvents; not above 65 °C |
| Neoprene | Hydrocarbons, aliphatic solvents, acids, alkalis | Aromatic/chlorinated solvents, ketones |
| Butyl rubber | Aggressive solvents (acetone), mineral acids, alcohols | — |
| Natural rubber (latex) | Dilute acids, strong alkalis | Fats, hydrocarbons, chlorinated solvents (check for latex allergy) |

### Glove Hygiene

- Remove gloves **before** touching door handles, phones, keyboards, or any uncontaminated surface.
- Do not wear gloves outside the laboratory.
- Wash hands thoroughly after removing gloves.

### Footwear and Clothing

- **No** sandals, open-toed shoes, short trousers, or short skirts in the laboratory.
- Closed, sturdy footwear is mandatory.

### Contact Lenses

- Wearing contact lenses in the laboratory is **strongly discouraged**. If unavoidable, safety goggles (not just glasses) must be worn over them.
- In case of chemical eye exposure, remove contact lenses immediately during washing and do not reinsert them.

### Respiratory Protection

- **FFP3 particulate masks**: required for powder or granule spills, as indicated in the relevant Safety Data Sheet (SDS).
- **Gas mask with appropriate filters**: required for liquid chemical spills, as indicated in the SDS.
- Replace filters per manufacturer instructions; if breathing resistance increases, replace immediately.

### Additional PPE

- **Face shields**: required when pouring or transferring corrosive materials or large volumes of dangerous liquids. A face shield does **not** replace eye protection — wear both.
- **Heat-resistant gloves** (leather or thermal-rated): for handling hot items or cryogenic materials.

## 2. General Workstation Management

- **Cleanliness**: workstations must be thoroughly cleaned before and after every use.
- **Stain prevention**: use laboratory paper under coloured reagents to prevent stains, unless the reagent can start a fire on the paper.
- **Glassware**: keep personal glassware at your station. Never leave dirty glassware in the sink.
- **Sink maintenance**: store sponges outside the sink after use, **don't leave anything in the sink**, Any glassware awaiting cleaning must be placed on your designated bench mat.
- **Windowsills**: must always be kept clear for safety.
- **Bench mats**: Users may leave their items (e.g., reagents, products, unwashed glassware) on a bench mat clearly labeled with their name. The owner bears sole responsibility for these items and must keep the area tidy and safe for all the lab (e.g., no open containers of hazardous materials). Touching items on a colleague's mat without explicit permission is strictly prohibited.
- **Reagents**: Store all reagents in the appropriate cabinets after use. **Never leave reagents unattended on the bench**. If a reagent is kept out for over one working day, update the inventory notes accordingly,specifying the name of the responsible user.

---

## 3. Unattended Operations

- **Supervision**: do not leave chemical reactions or stirring mixtures unattended.
- **Documentation**: if you must leave, leave a written note with your **surname** and the **reagents** used.
- **Heating**: never leave hot plates unattended; mark them with a "Caution: Hot Plate" note.

## 4. Specialised Areas

### Balance Room

- **Cleaning**: both the balance and the workbench must be cleaned using the provided spray bottle.
- **Safety**: high cleanliness standards are mandatory due to potentially toxic substances.
- **Shutdown**: turn off the balance after use.

### Fume Hoods

- **Allocation**: one hood is dedicated to the photocatalytic reactor (primarily RM), and the other is for the TBM group.
- **Coordination**: coordinate in advance for extended usage.
- **Safety**:
    - Verify the aspiration is functioning **before** starting any operation.
    - Keep the glass sash lowered as much as possible during work.
    - Position materials at least **15–20 cm** from the front opening and toward the back of the hood.
    - Do **not** obstruct the airflow along the work surface: raise bulky equipment ≥ 5 cm from the surface and keep items away from intake vents.
    - Avoid creating air currents near the hood — do not open doors or windows in the vicinity and minimise foot traffic.
    - Clean the workstation before and after use.
- **Storage**: do **not** use hoods to store reagents or samples. Keep only the materials strictly necessary for the current operation inside.
- **Night protocol**: turn off the hood via the main switch at the end of the day to avoid night alarms.

## 5. Shared Equipment

### Centrifuge and Ultrasonic Baths

- **Reservations**: use the weekly schedule; cancel promptly if not needed.
- **Maintenance**: check for liquid leaks after use.
- **Baths**: ensure water is clear; if dirty, wipe the container and refill with fresh Elix water.

### Ovens

- **Oven 1** [left, 100°C]: dedicated to analytical glassware; the upper shelf is strictly for EPR cells.
- **Oven 2** [right, 60°C]: dedicated to synthesis glassware and samples.
- **Morning routine**: empty all ovens every morning.

### Automatic Pipettes

- **Training**: use only after proper training, during volume selection don't exceed the upper and lower limits indicated on the pipette. 
- **Handling**: clean the tip ejector and cone after use.
- **Storage**: never leave pipettes horizontal; always store them vertically.

## 6. Chemical Handling and Storage

### General Rules

- Read the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) **before** using any unfamiliar substance.
- Use the minimum quantity necessary for the experiment.
- Handle volatile or toxic substances inside a fume hood.
- Never pipette by mouth.
- Do not return unused chemicals to stock bottles — dispose of surplus appropriately.

### Storage

- Store all chemicals in their **original containers**. If a transfer is necessary, the new container must carry the same label information as the original (substance name, pictograms, H/P statements, supplier data).
- Every chemical in the lab must have its SDS stored in a known, accessible location.
- Keep only the quantities **strictly necessary for current work** in the laboratory; bulk stocks belong in the dedicated reagent storage room.

### Where NOT to Store Chemicals

- On the floor.
- On open workbenches.
- Inside fume hoods (hoods are for active work, not storage).

### Storage Cabinets

- **Flammable liquids** → fire-resistant safety cabinets (Type 90, UNI EN 14470-1). Maximum **20 L** of flammable liquids per fire compartment.
- **Corrosive substances (acids and bases)** → ventilated cabinets with external aspiration. Acids and bases must be stored **separately**.
- **Toxic substances** → cabinets with external aspiration and appropriate warning signage.
- Each cabinet must display a list of contents, hazard indicators, and the date of the last update.

### Shelf Arrangement

- Place corrosive, caustic, and irritant agents **below eye level**.
- Use lower shelves for larger containers and more hazardous substances.
- Do not stack or overload shelves.
- Protect from direct sunlight and heat sources.
- Temperature-sensitive substances must be refrigerated as per SDS. Flammable liquids requiring refrigeration must go in an **explosion-proof** refrigerator.

### Incompatibilities — Never Store Together

| Substance | Incompatible With |
|---|---|
| Acetylene | Fluorine, chlorine, bromine, silver, copper, mercury |
| Oxygen (compressed) | Oils, fats, hydrogen, flammable liquids and gases |
| Organic peroxides | Inorganic and organic acids |
| Cyanides | Acids (generates HCN) |
| Sulphides | Acids (generates H₂S) |
| Hypochlorites | Acids (generates Cl₂) |
| Alkali metals (Na, K, Li) | Water, halogenated solvents |
| Strong oxidisers | Flammable or organic materials |

## 8. Waste Disposal

### Waste Categories (CER Codes)

| CER Code | Category |
|---|---|
| 06.01.06* | Acids |
| 06.02.05* | Bases |
| 06.04.05* | Waste containing heavy metals |
| 07.07.03* | Halogenated organic solvents, washing solutions, mother liquors |
| 07.07.04* | Other (non-halogenated) organic solvents, washing solutions, mother liquors |
| 15.02.02* | Absorbents, filter materials, rags, and protective clothing contaminated with hazardous substances |

### Collection Rules

- Sort waste by category. **Never** mix incompatible waste streams.
- Use **one container per waste type** at a time.
- Transfer waste **under the fume hood** while wearing appropriate PPE.
- Keep waste containers inside a **containment tray** whose volume is at least equal to the capacity of the largest container.
- Record every disposal in the **waste book**, noting the CER code, substance, quantity, and date.
- When a container is full, complete the form on the departmental website.

### General Precautions

- **Never** pour chemicals down the drain unless explicitly permitted by institutional waste policy.
- Label all waste containers clearly with: contents, CER code, hazard characteristics, and accumulation start date.
- Note low stock levels of consumables (plasticware, gloves, reagents) on the Lab 1 door list.
- Notify coordinators early — orders can take over a month to arrive.

## 9. Sample Storage and Exit

- **Labelling**: all samples in the fridge or on benches must show your **surname**, **date**, and **content**.
- **Containers**: do not use volumetric glassware for storage; transfer to Falcon tubes or bottles.
- **Final check**: dispose of dirty materials and gloves before leaving.
- **Lights**: the last operator to leave must turn off the lights.

## 10. Spill Procedures

### Spill Size Classification

| Size | Volume | Who Intervenes |
|---|---|---|
| Small | < 300 mL | Lab responsible (RADRL) and present staff |
| Medium | 300 mL – 5 L | Lab responsible (RADRL) and present staff |
| Large | > 5 L | **External emergency services** (Vigili del Fuoco) — evacuate and call immediately |

### General Response Steps

1. **Read** SDS Section 6 ("Measures in case of accidental release") for the specific substance.
2. **Put on PPE**: lab coat or Tyvek suit, waterproof shoe covers, chemical-compatible gloves (double if needed), safety goggles, respiratory protection as indicated in the SDS (FFP3 for powders, gas mask for liquids).
3. **Alert** the lab supervisor (in person or by phone).
4. **Evacuate** the laboratory if the spill involves a volatile or highly toxic liquid; alert everyone present.
5. **Open windows** to ventilate (dilution effect).
6. **Close the laboratory door** and post a sign: *"Do not enter — hazardous substance cleanup in progress."*

### Liquid Spill Cleanup

1. Distribute absorbent powder from the **outside inward** in a slow circular motion.
2. Cover with absorbent cloth or paper from the spill kit.
3. Wait for the absorbent to solidify.
4. Remove with the single-use spatula and shovel from the kit.
5. **Broken glass**: never touch with bare hands — use a shovel or forceps.
6. Collect all contaminated materials and dispose as hazardous waste (CER 15.02.02*).
7. If indicated in the SDS, wash the area with water.
8. Dry and verify the cleaned surface is not slippery.
9. Discard used gloves and disposable masks together with contaminated materials.

### Powder Spill Cleanup

- Follow the same evacuation and PPE steps as for liquid spills.
- Do **not** sweep dry or use a regular vacuum cleaner. Clean **only** with a cloth dampened with water and sodium hypochlorite solution, wiping from the cleanest area toward the most contaminated.
- All cloths go in a double plastic bag and are disposed as hazardous waste (CER 15.02.02*).

## 11. Emergency Procedures

### Fire

1. **Do not panic.** Move away from the fire zone; abandon personal belongings.
2. **If heavy smoke**: walk low near the floor where oxygen concentration is higher; cover nose and mouth with a wet cloth.
3. **Move along walls** with the **back of your hand** facing forward (if you touch a live electrical wire, the reflex contraction will pull your hand away).
4. Use **stairs only** — never elevators. Do **not** go upstairs (smoke rises).
5. Do **not** open doors that feel hot.
6. Follow evacuation routes to the designated **assembly point**.
7. Call emergency services.

#### Pre-Alarm Lab Securing (if time permits)

- Store all flammable substances in fire cabinets.
- Store all toxic/harmful substances in safety cabinets.
- Close flammable and oxidising gas cylinders.
- Close fume hoods.
- Secure chemical waste containers.
- Disconnect general electrical power.
- Close doors and windows.

#### Fire Extinguisher Selection

| Fire Class | Fuel | Suitable Agent |
|---|---|---|
| A | Solids (wood, paper, rubber) | Water, foam, powder |
| B | Liquids (petrol, oils, solvents) | Foam, powder, CO₂ |
| C | Gases (methane, propane) | Powder, CO₂ |
| D | Metals (magnesium, aluminium) | Special metal powders (NaCl) |
| Electrical equipment | — | CO₂ or powder (never water before removing power) |

### Chemical Exposure

#### Eye Contact

1. Wash the affected eye immediately for **at least 15 minutes** at the eyewash station, keeping the eye open and flushing from the inner corner outward to avoid contaminating the other eye.
2. Remove contact lenses during washing; **do not** reinsert them.
3. Cover the injured eye with sterile gauze.
4. Go to the emergency room for an ophthalmological examination, bringing the SDS.
5. **Do not** apply eye drops or ointments before the medical examination.

#### Skin Contact

1. Identify the substance.
2. Read SDS Section 4 ("First aid measures").
3. Remove contaminated clothing and PPE.
4. Wash immediately with **water only**. **Never use solvents** to clean the skin — they can alter the lipid barrier and accelerate absorption.
5. Seek medical attention if needed, bringing the SDS.

#### Inhalation

1. Identify the substance inhaled.
2. Move the affected person to **fresh air**.
3. Lay the person supine; loosen any clothing that restricts breathing; monitor vital signs (pulse, breathing, consciousness).
4. Call emergency services (118) if necessary.
5. Check the SDS for possible delayed health effects.

#### Accidental Ingestion

1. Identify the substance ingested.
2. Read SDS Section 4.
3. Contact the **Poison Control Centre** (Centro Antiveleni, Torino – Molinette: **011 663 7637**), providing: substance name, estimated quantity, time of ingestion, person's condition (conscious? vomiting?), approximate age and weight.
4. **Do NOT induce vomiting** unless explicitly instructed by the SDS or the Poison Control Centre — vomiting can cause further damage to an already injured gastrointestinal tract.
5. **Do NOT give milk** — fats can accelerate gastric absorption of the chemical.
6. **Do NOT** administer any medication before medical consultation.
7. If vomiting occurs spontaneously, do not try to stop it.
8. Go to the emergency room with the SDS.
